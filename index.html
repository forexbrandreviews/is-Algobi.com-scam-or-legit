Top Red Flags We Observed

  • No verifiable license number or regulator link on the website’s footer or legal pages.
  • Anonymous ownership and vague company address details.
  • Unrealistic profit promises and “guaranteed returns” marketing.
  • Deposit pressure from “account managers” via chat/phone.
  • Withdrawal obstruction: sudden “tax/clearance fee” demands before releasing funds.
  • Dashboard manipulation: profits displayed that cannot be withdrawn.

Why Novice Traders Are Targeted

Beginners are lured by screenshots, fake testimonials, Telegram groups, and “limited-time bonuses.” Remember: bonuses ≠ profits and often come with hidden terms blocking withdrawals.

How the Typical Scam Playbook Works

  1. Trust hook: Social media ads or DMs promise an “AI bot” or “guaranteed daily ROI.”
  2. First deposit: Small gains appear on the dashboard to build confidence.
  3. Upsell: You’re told to “unlock VIP” or “double your profits” with a larger deposit.
  4. Block: When you request a withdrawal, extra “tax/verification fees” appear.
  5. Silence: If you refuse, support stops responding or your account is frozen.

Tip: A legitimate broker won’t require extra payments to release your funds.

Algobi.com Reviews: What Users Report

What To Collect If You Were Affected

  • Screenshots of deposits, balances, and error messages.
  • All email/chat transcripts with “account managers”.
  • Bank/crypto transaction hashes, dates, and amounts.
  • Any license numbers or company names you were provided.

Need Help? Speak to a Real Person

If you think you’ve been defrauded, act quickly:

  • Cease further deposits and block contact from pushy agents.
  • Document everything; keep communications in one folder.
  • Inform your bank/exchange about potential fraud and request guidance.
  • Report to your local cybercrime or financial regulator portal.
